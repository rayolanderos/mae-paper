Novel methods to PPD screening

No other new mother application provides this simple and effective method for tracking new baby details while being passively screened for symptoms of PPD.

The convenience of talking to Alexa opens a new path for checking in with people who would otherwise avoid doing tedious journaling. By analyzing the userâ€™s change in personality and tone, rather than asking standard PPD rating questions during a routine healthcare visit, our application has the potential to determine exact onset of the change in behavior, which can be important for PPD diagnosis and treatment. By providing a separate easy-to-use journaling function for new mothers, the application encourages more consistent and widespread usage by new mothers, thereby helping to diagnose and prevent more cases of PPD.

Other applications specifically for PPD exist but do not attempt to analyze the mother's behavior using advanced cognitive applications or provide these additional features to encourage usage by a greater number of users over a longer period of time.

Future work

Further research is needed, including clinical trials to determine whether Mae accurately screens for PPD. In its current proof of concept version, it can only be seen as a supportive tool for new mothers and should not be used as a diagnostic tool. Using Mae in conjunction with the traditional questionnare screening tools to identify correlation would be worthwhile work. Additionally, usability tests with larger groups of users to determine usability of the voice commands to ensure that the solution provides the ease of use that Mae intends to provide for new mothers.
